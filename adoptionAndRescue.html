Our Success Stories

The Rescue of Pookey the Donkey

Pookey was discovered in an extremely dire condition on a farm, barely clinging to life. When our team encountered him in 2010, his survival seemed highly unlikely. His situation represented the most severe neglect the welfare team had ever witnessed. Pookey suffered from a skin infection in his face, and shockingly, his owners had opted to worsen his condition by applying industrial-strength bleach to his wounds. They callously tethered him to a pole, abandoning him to face an imminent demise. After his rescue, Pookey received crucial veterinary care that gradually healed his wounds. However, it is the consistent care provided over months and years that has been instrumental in fostering Pookey's confidence and rebuilding his trust in humans.

The Rescue of Tootsie the Donkey

Dimona, a city surrounded by desert, occasionally sees sick and injured donkeys venturing in search of food and water. The donkey's innate curiosity often leads them into trouble, prompting local Bedouins to tie their legs to prevent accidents on roads. A mare, desperately seeking sustenance, walked into Dimona with her legs bound, likely causing injury. Three boys from Dimona, Lior, Moshe, and Ilay, discovered her and noticed her distress. Concerned, they freed her, fed her, provided water, and promptly reported the situation to Dr. Natalia Forost, the municipal vet. Dr. Natalia, accompanied by Haim Verzky, verified the mare's condition and urgently contacted us. Upon arrival, we found her miraculously surviving after days of wandering.

The Rescue of Elvis the Donkey

Elvis was found in the rugged expanse of the West Bank, where day after grueling day, he bore the burden of hauling unfathomably heavy cargo for a staggering 12 hours. When exhaustion overcame him, the lash of cruelty compelled him to press on. One fateful day, a young girl, compelled by an invisible force of empathy, stumbled upon this heart-wrenching spectacle. Unable to bear witness to such injustice, she bravely confronted the owners. Their chilling response echoed with indifference, challenging her to buy the donkey's freedom if she cared so much. The girl sprinted home, emptied her savings and returned to the place of despair. She exchanged currency for liberation, breaking the chains that bound Elvis to his torment. This young savior, whose identity remainees anonymous, emerged as a beacon of hope for Elvis. She reached out to us, transforming from a mere observer into Elvis's steadfast sponsor.
